<p><p>The mycotoxin binder market is categorized into several types, including raw clay, zeolites, aluminosilicates, and polysaccharides. Raw clay serves as a natural absorbent, trapping mycotoxins in feed. Zeolites are crystalline structures that effectively bind a range of toxins due to their porous nature. Aluminosilicates, similar to zeolites, offer excellent adsorption properties. Polysaccharides, derived from natural sources, provide a unique mechanism to bind mycotoxins while promoting gut health. Each type plays a crucial role in ensuring food safety and animal health.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Dispersive</li><li>Cohesive</li><li>Combined</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Ophthalmic Viscosurgical Devices (OVDs) are classified into three main types: dispersive, cohesive, and combined. Dispersive OVDs provide extensive lubrication and create a protective barrier during cataract surgery, minimizing endothelial cell damage. Cohesive OVDs, on the other hand, effectively maintain space and facilitate tactics like capsule filling with better visibility and ease of removal. Combined OVDs merge the properties of both types, offering enhanced performance for various surgical procedures, optimizing space maintenance and tissue protection simultaneously.</p></p>

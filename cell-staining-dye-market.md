<p><p>The cell staining dye market is categorized into three main types: acidic, basic, and neutral cell stains. Acidic stains typically bind to positively charged cellular components, highlighting structures like nuclei, while basic stains interact with negatively charged components, enhancing the visibility of cell membranes and cytoplasm. Neutral stains exhibit a balanced affinity for both types, providing a general overview of cellular morphology. Each type serves distinct purposes in biological research, diagnostics, and clinical applications, facilitating microscopic examination and analysis of cells.</p></p>
